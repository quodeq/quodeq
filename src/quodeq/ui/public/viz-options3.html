Actionable Visualizations — "Where Should I Fix First?"

Focused on surfacing hotspots and priorities from codebase quality data.

1

Risk Matrix — Severity × Volume

Best for: Quick triage. X-axis = total violations, Y-axis = worst severity score. Top-right = urgent (many + severe). Bottom-left = low priority. Each bubble is a file/folder. Size = total findings. Instantly shows what to fix first.

2

Stacked Bar Chart — Files Ranked by Severity

Best for: Top-N offenders list. Files sorted by total violations. Each bar split into critical (red), major (orange), minor (yellow) segments. Health rate shown as a thin green bar on the right. Instantly see which files need the most work.

3

Radar / Spider Chart — Per-Dimension Health

Best for: Dimension comparison. Each axis = one quality dimension. Filled area shows health rate (0-100%). Multiple overlaid shapes compare folders. Immediately see which dimensions are weakest. Works great with the dimension filter.

4

Waffle Chart — Proportional Overview

Best for: At-a-glance proportion. Each small square = one finding. Critical = red, major = orange, minor = yellow, compliance = green. Grouped by folder. Shows both the proportion of good vs bad AND the absolute volume. Very intuitive.

5

Dot Strip — Every Violation Visible

Best for: Granular view. Each dot = one violation. Rows = files. Dots colored by severity, positioned by line number. See exactly where violations cluster within files. Hover for details. Like a minimap of your codebase issues.

6

Severity Breakdown — Nested Bars with Health Indicator

Best for: Folder-level summary. Folders as rows, each with an expandable bar showing severity breakdown. Right side shows health rate as a progress bar. Combines the heat grid's data density with visual clarity.
